Facebook: The reason for it's success

How has it become an international force

October 01

Over the past two decades, the rise in social networking sites helping people stay in touch and share information with others has led Facebook to become a company that grew rapidly. Facebook was created exactly when social networking was on a rise, the internet was being available to everyone during that period.

Facebook stood apart from the rest of its competitors like MySpace and Friendster because it was easily available, had better innovations for a social networking site. Facebook's efficiency in handling an increasing volume of users and an increasing network of people made Facebook a global business. Innovations within the application allowed greater use of Facebook. It was ready for people all over the world, with every other language within it. Having software engineers create more for Facebook keeping consumers in mind made the company a go-forward company. Facebook undoubtedly is a huge success. It adds a large variety of features, adjustments and has launched a variety of other apps that also became a success.

There are always certain risks when something has a steep rise. Facebook certainly faces risks that mostly have to do with the revenue of the company. Secluding revenues, its competitors, new technologies, and privacy concerns are a risk to the ever-growing company. Many advertisers are hesitant to advertise on social networking applications because the content is all user-generated. Facebook's collection of personal user data remains an issue that gives rise to questions regarding privacy regulations.
